The archive documents the partition and post-partition narratives of Bahawalpuri Hindus in India, an ethno-linguistic minority whose partition narratives remain invisible and undocumented in the corpus of partition scholarship. Bahawalpuri Hindus migrated to India from Bahawalpur State during 1947 partition and remain a minor community whose voices and narratives have not been documented or deliberated upon in the historical and cultural studies of 1947 partition. Bahawalpuri Hindus are historically a cultural-linguistic community whose cultural and linguistic heritage is swiftly disappearing in India. The intellectuals and independent writers in the community are making efforts to revive their language Saraiki (previously known by various local names such as Riyasti, Multani, Jatki, Bahawalpuri) within the community. Our digital preservation project in collaboration with the community is recording their partition testimonies and archiving the available literary artefacts and speech samples of old Saraiki spoken by few partition survivors in India.
